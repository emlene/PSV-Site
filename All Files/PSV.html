RIZAL CAMPUS

About Campus


PSU Rizal traces its early beginnings in 2000 by virtue of a Sangguniang Bayan Resolution authored by late councilor Daniel Tacardon—the Committee Chair on Education—requesting Palawan State University to open the campus. The said resolution had earned the unanimous support of the entire council under the gavel of former Vice-Mayor Wendelina Usodan. The ate Mayor Samson Degillo commissioned some personnel from his office to study the proposal and on that same year, the resolution was approved paving the way for the much-awaited creation of Palawan State University College of Community Resources Development Rizal Campus. With the backing of Dr. Jesus Apdon, then Dean of CCRD Campuses and the Vice President for Finance of Palawan State University Desdichado Villasario and all other university officials, the Memorandum of Agreement between the Local Government Unit of Rizal, the Parents and Teachers Association, and the Palawan State University was finally signed by Mayor Samson I. Degillo, PTA President Redemson Trongco and Dr. Teresita L. Salva on the 22nd day of June 2000. Finally, on June 05, 2000, after a series of meetings, the first class of the local campus started under the directorship of Prof. Danilo Esmerio, who served the local campus until 2007 under the shed of a borrowed vacated municipal building of the local government at Tarampitao Point, a former US Naval Base. Some classes were also held under the roof of the dilapidated Long Range Aid to Navigation (LORAN) facility of the US Navy in Tarampitao Point, particularly known today as Base, referring to the Naval Base. The campus remained as tenant in these facilities until the donation of the old municipal building including the land area in favor of DEPED forced the university to transfer to its new site at Sitio Balite which was donated by the local government and the family of Renato Tabuzo. In November 2013, the campus finally re-located its campus under the directorship of Prof. Melodia Salimbagat. PSU Rizal received one unit of single-classroom school building from then Congressman of the 2nd District of Palawan, Atty. Victorino Dennis Socrates; another one unit of single-classroom building from Congressman Eric Abueg; one unit of 4-classroom building from Governor Jose Alvarez. Still during the stint of Prof. Salimbagat, the local campus was able to build one unit of 4-classroom school building funded under the fiduciary funds of the local campus. The local government also added one unit of 2-classroom school building. Within the span of three years, the Parents Teachers Association has also completed and contributed one unit of single-classroom computer laboratory building.

Palawan State University is committed to upgrade the people's quality of life by providing education opportunities through excellent instruction, research and innovation, extension, production service, and transnational collaborations.
